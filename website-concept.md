## 1. Brand Positioning Statement & Value Proposition

### Positioning Statement (one sentence)
**Dr. Jahangir Kabir is Bangladesh's trusted lifestyle-medicine educator, guiding over a million people toward preventive, evidence-informed everyday health through honest education, traceable organic nutrition, and sustainable living — never quick fixes.**

### Value Proposition
For health-conscious Bengalis at home and abroad who are overwhelmed by conflicting health advice and wary of empty promises, the JK Lifestyle ecosystem offers **credible, MBBS-grounded lifestyle education** paired with **certified-traceable products** — so that small, sustainable daily habits become lasting wellbeing, always alongside (never instead of) conventional medical care.

### Supporting Pillars
- **Credibility through honesty** — MBBS (Chittagong Medical College, 2000), 25+ years dedicated to lifestyle medicine, and a refusal to over-claim. Authority earned, not advertised.
- **Education before transaction** — public seminars, published books, and free content come first; products and programs support the learning, not the reverse.
- **Traceability as trust** — Bioagricert-certified organic products with batch tracking and transparent sourcing; openness is the deliberate answer to past scrutiny.
- **A connected ecosystem** — five organizations (Health Revolution, Ultimate Organic Life, JK Fitness Arena, JK Food Arena, JK Prokriti Krishi) covering education, nutrition, movement, and sustainable agriculture under one coherent philosophy.
- **Five Pillars framework** — Nutrition, Movement, Sleep, Stress, and Daily Habits as a memorable, repeatable structure for preventive health.

## 2. Target Audience Personas

### Persona 1 — "Rehana, the Preventive Homemaker" (Dhaka, 42)
- **Profile:** Urban Bangladeshi mother, manages family meals and health, browses primarily on a mobile phone, reads in Bangla first.
- **Goals:** Cook healthier for her family, understand fasting and nutrition safely, source trustworthy organic staples (oils, salt, rice).
- **Objections:** "Is this another influencer selling miracle cures?" / "Are these organic claims real or just marketing?"
- **What converts her:** Plain-Bangla explanations, visible Bioagricert certification and batch traceability, disclaimers that show honesty rather than hype.

### Persona 2 — "Tanvir, the Diaspora Professional" (London/Toronto, 35)
- **Profile:** Second-generation or expatriate Bengali, bilingual but English-dominant, time-poor, health-literate, skeptical of unregulated health content.
- **Goals:** Manage metabolic risk and weight through structured habits; reconnect with culturally familiar, credible health guidance; possibly ship/recommend products to family back home.
- **Objections:** "Where are the credentials and the evidence?" / "I won't trust anyone who promises to 'cure' anything."
- **What converts him:** Clear MBBS + 25-year track record, evidence-informed and compliant tone, citable Five Pillars framework, professional editorial design that signals legitimacy.

### Persona 3 — "Dr. Anika, the Allied Professional / Partner" (Chattogram, 38)
- **Profile:** Nutritionist, trainer, clinic, or wholesale buyer evaluating Dr. Kabir's ecosystem for collaboration, referral, or stocking products.
- **Goals:** Verify legitimacy, understand the organization structure, assess product certification and program scope for partnership.
- **Objections:** "Does this brand stay within ethical/regulatory bounds?" / "Is the supply chain genuinely certified and traceable?"
- **What converts her:** Transparent ecosystem map, certification documentation, compliant claims, clear contact and partnership pathways.

## 3. Creative Direction & Art Direction

### Mood
"**Quiet medical authority meets editorial warmth.**" The current "luxury editorial wellness" aesthetic is retained but matured: less ornamental, more clinical confidence. The feeling should be a trusted physician's consultation room reimagined as a beautifully published health journal — calm, precise, generous with whitespace, never flashy.

### Evolving Green + Gold Toward Maximum Medical Credibility
- **Keep** deep forest green (#122319, #1a3725) as the anchor — it reads as natural, established, and serious.
- **Demote gold (#c9a05c) to an accent**, not a dominant tone. Heavy gold reads "luxury sales"; restrained gold reads "considered quality." Use it for thin rules, small icons, and key CTAs only.
- **Introduce a clinical-trust neutral layer:** generous cream/off-white (#f4efe4) backgrounds plus a cool clean white for "evidence" zones (certifications, disclaimers, data, product specs). White space itself signals medical clarity.
- **Add a soft supporting sage/clinical-teal tint** (e.g., a muted desaturated green-grey) for informational panels and badges, distinct from the rich brand green, to differentiate "trust/data" content from "brand/emotion" content.
- **Reserve Great Vibes script** for a single signature moment (e.g., a tagline or his name flourish) — overuse undermines credibility.

### Typography Direction
- **Playfair Display** — headings only, used sparingly and large; conveys editorial gravitas.
- **Inter** — all body, UI, data, labels, disclaimers; maximum legibility on mobile.
- **Bengali support is mandatory:** pair with a high-quality Bengali web font (e.g., **Noto Serif Bengali** for headings, **Noto Sans Bengali / Hind Siliguri** for body). Never render Bangla in a fallback system font. Match weight/rhythm so bilingual pages feel unified.

### Imagery Direction
- **Hero / authority:** the authentic podium-with-laptop seminar photo — real educator credibility beats stock.
- **Fitness:** the bench-press "LIFESTYLE" apparel athlete — for JK Fitness Arena and Movement pillar.
- **Products:** clean white-background bottle shots (front + back labels) — let labels and certification be visible and legible; this transparency is a feature.
- **Texture & lifestyle:** real ingredients, real Bangladeshi context, sustainable-agriculture/field imagery for JK Prokriti Krishi.
- **People:** authentic, consented, diverse-age Bengali faces for testimonials.

### Do / Don't List
**Do**
- Use real, authentic photography of Dr. Kabir and the ecosystem.
- Give certifications, disclaimers, and data their own clean, well-designed space.
- Keep layouts airy, mobile-first, fast-loading (optimize images for variable networks).
- Let typography and whitespace carry the "premium" feeling.

**Don't**
- Don't drown the design in gold or heavy gradients — it cheapens trust.
- Don't use "miracle/cure/before-after disease" visual tropes or sensational badges.
- Don't bury disclaimers in tiny grey footnotes; design them as honest, visible elements.
- Don't use stocky Western "wellness" clichés that ignore the Bengali audience.
- Don't render Bangla in low-quality fallback fonts.

## 4. Global Content & Tone-of-Voice Guidelines

### Voice Attributes
- **Credible, not boastful** — state true facts (MBBS, 25+ years, 1,000,000+ reached, founder of five organizations, published books) plainly; let them speak.
- **Educational, not promotional** — explain mechanisms and habits; teach first.
- **Humble & compliant** — "may support," "can help," "lifestyle education," "preventive," "under medical supervision."
- **Warm & culturally fluent** — bilingual, respectful, accessible to a non-clinical reader.

### Hard Rules Modeled in Copy
- **Never** mention or imply any postgraduate or specialist title. The only stated degree is **MBBS, Chittagong Medical College, 2000**.
- **Never** use: "cure," "reverse diabetes in 7 days," "guaranteed," "miracle," "permanent solution," or any disease-cure claim.
- **Always** clarify that programs are **educational and complementary**, that **serious or acute conditions require conventional medical care**, and include a **"consult your physician"** note wherever health outcomes are discussed.
- **Testimonials** show *lifestyle improvements only*, are consented, and carry **"individual results vary."**

### Example Compliant Headline Rewrites
| ❌ Non-compliant (never use) | ✅ Compliant rewrite (the voice) |
|---|---|
| "Reverse your diabetes in 7 days — guaranteed!" | "Learn lifestyle habits that may support healthy blood-sugar management — alongside your doctor's care." |
| "Dr. Kabir, [fabricated specialist title], cures metabolic disease" | "Dr. Jahangir Kabir, MBBS — 25+ years educating Bangladesh on preventive lifestyle medicine." |
| "The miracle oil that melts fat permanently" | "Certified organic Extra Virgin Coconut Oil — traceable from source, part of a balanced lifestyle." |
| "Our program is the permanent solution to obesity" | "An educational program to help you build sustainable daily habits for long-term wellbeing." |
| "Patients GUARANTEED to heal with our fasting protocol" | "Intermittent-fasting education explained simply — please consult your physician before starting." |

### 2. Typography System

#### 2.1 Font Families

| Role | Latin font | Bengali font | Stack |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Headings | Playfair Display | **Noto Serif Bengali** | `'Playfair Display','Noto Serif Bengali',Georgia,serif` |
| Body / UI / Data | Inter | **Hind Siliguri** (fallback Noto Sans Bengali) | `'Inter','Hind Siliguri','Noto Sans Bengali',system-ui,sans-serif` |
| Script accent (one moment) | Great Vibes | — (Latin only) | `'Great Vibes',cursive` |

> Bengali is **never** rendered in a system fallback. When the document/`:lang(bn)` is Bengali, headings switch to Noto Serif Bengali (matched optical weight) and body to Hind Siliguri. Load Bengali subsets via `unicode-range` so Latin-only pages don't pay the byte cost — critical for variable mobile networks. Apply `font-display: swap`.

#### 2.2 Type Scale (fluid, mobile-first)

Fluid between **375px** and **1440px** viewports. `clamp(min, preferred, max)` — preferred derived as `min + (max−min) × (100vw−375px)/(1440−375px)`, expressed in `rem + vw`. Base = 16px = 1rem.

# 3. Responsive Design Specification

## Responsive Design System — Breakpoints, Grid, Fluid Type & Performance

This section defines the responsive behavior for every page in the JK Lifestyle / Dr. Jahangir Kabir site. It is mobile-first by default, bilingual-aware (Bangla/English share the same layout engine), and tuned for variable Bangladeshi network speeds.

---

### Breakpoint Map

| Token | Range | Label | Primary device | Grid columns | Container max-width |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `--bp-xs` | **320–767px** | Mobile | Phones (portrait) | 4 | 100% (fluid) |
| `--bp-sm` | **768–1023px** | Tablet | Tablets, large phones (landscape) | 8 | 720px |
| `--bp-md` | **1024–1439px** | Laptop | Small laptops | 12 | 960px |
| `--bp-lg` | **1440–1919px** | Desktop | Large laptops, monitors | 12 | 1280px |
| `--bp-xl` | **1920px+** | Ultra-wide | Wide & 4K monitors | 12 | 1440px (centered, capped) |

```css
:root {
  /* Min-width breakpoints — mobile-first, all media queries are min-width */
  --bp-sm: 768px;
  --bp-md: 1024px;
  --bp-lg: 1440px;
  --bp-xl: 1920px;
}
```

**Methodology.** Base styles are authored for 320px with no media query. Each breakpoint is an additive `min-width` layer — we never write `max-width` queries except for the rare "mobile-only" override (e.g., off-canvas drawer). This keeps the cascade predictable and ships the smallest CSS first to slow connections. The content never breaks below 320px; below that, horizontal scroll is accepted rather than a sixth breakpoint.

---

### Container Max-Widths & Gutters

The page is governed by one `.container` primitive. Gutters (outer page padding) and the inter-column gap scale with viewport. Content max-width is intentionally capped well below the viewport on ultra-wide so long-form Bangla/English reading measure stays comfortable (~66–75ch).

| Breakpoint | Container max-width | Side gutter (padding-inline) | Column gap | Reading measure cap (prose) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mobile (320–767) | 100% | 16px (20px ≥ 480px) | 16px | 100% |
| Tablet (768–1023) | 720px | 24px | 20px | 68ch |
| Laptop (1024–1439) | 960px | 32px | 24px | 70ch |
| Desktop (1440–1919) | 1280px | 40px | 24px | 72ch |
| Ultra-wide (1920+) | 1440px | 48px | 32px | 75ch |

```css
.container {
  width: 100%;
  max-width: var(--container-max, 100%);
  margin-inline: auto;
  padding-inline: var(--gutter, 16px);
}
@media (min-width: 480px)  { :root { --gutter: 20px; } }
@media (min-width: 768px)  { :root { --container-max: 720px;  --gutter: 24px; --grid-gap: 20px; } }
@media (min-width: 1024px) { :root { --container-max: 960px;  --gutter: 32px; --grid-gap: 24px; } }
@media (min-width: 1440px) { :root { --container-max: 1280px; --gutter: 40px; } }
@media (min-width: 1920px) { :root { --container-max: 1440px; --gutter: 48px; --grid-gap: 32px; } }

/* Narrow variant for long-form prose (Blog article, About biography) */
.container--prose { max-width: min(72ch, var(--container-max)); }
```

### Grid System

A single CSS Grid scaffold (`.grid`) provides 4 → 8 → 12 columns. Components declare span in column units and reflow by re-spanning at each breakpoint rather than by re-nesting markup — one DOM, many layouts.

```css
.grid {
  display: grid;
  gap: var(--grid-gap, 16px);
  grid-template-columns: repeat(4, 1fr); /* mobile base: 4 col */
}
@media (min-width: 768px)  { .grid { grid-template-columns: repeat(8, 1fr);  } }
@media (min-width: 1024px) { .grid { grid-template-columns: repeat(12, 1fr); } }

/* Span helpers, e.g. a card that is full-width mobile, half tablet, third laptop */
.col-full   { grid-column: 1 / -1; }
@media (min-width: 768px)  { .col-sm-4 { grid-column: span 4; } }   /* half of 8 */
@media (min-width: 1024px) { .col-md-4 { grid-column: span 4; } }   /* third of 12 */
@media (min-width: 1024px) { .col-md-3 { grid-column: span 3; } }   /* quarter of 12 */
```

| Breakpoint | Columns | Typical content layout |
|---|---|---|
| Mobile | 4 | Single stacked column; cards span all 4 |
| Tablet | 8 | 2-up cards (span 4); sidebar+main (span 3 / span 5) |
| Laptop | 12 | 3-up (span 4) or 4-up (span 3) card rows |
| Desktop | 12 | Same 12-col logic, wider gutters/gap |
| Ultra-wide | 12 | Same grid, capped container — extra space becomes margin, not more columns |

### Fluid Typography

Type scales smoothly with `clamp(MIN, PREFERRED, MAX)` so we never ship more than one heading size per element across breakpoints. Preferred terms are tuned for a 320 → 1440px fluid range. **Bengali runs ~0.06em larger** at the same nominal size for matching x-height/legibility, handled via a per-language multiplier on the root.

### Touch Targets & Input Ergonomics

- Minimum interactive target **44×44px** (Apple HIG / WCAG 2.5.5 floor); primary CTAs and nav items use **48px** height with ≥8px spacing so thumbs don't mis-tap.
- Language toggle (বাংলা/EN), hamburger, and sticky-bottom-bar icons are **48×48px** tap zones even when the visible glyph is smaller.
- Form fields: **48px** min height, **16px** font-size minimum (prevents iOS auto-zoom), 12px vertical rhythm between fields.
- Hover-only affordances always have a tap/focus equivalent; `:focus-visible` rings (2px gold `#c9a05c` on green, or 2px green on cream) are never removed.

}

### Mobile Performance Budget

Targets reflect a mid-range Android on a 3G/slow-4G Bangladeshi connection (the dominant real-world case), measured at p75 field data.

| Metric | Target (p75, mobile) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **LCP** | **≤ 2.5s** | LCP element = hero podium image (or hero H1 if image deferred) |
| **CLS** | **≤ 0.05** | Every image/embed has reserved `aspect-ratio`/`width`/`height` |
| **INP** | **≤ 200ms** | Drawer toggle, language switch, carousel must respond instantly |
| **TTFB** | **≤ 0.8s** | Edge cache / CDN near Dhaka + diaspora regions |
| **Total transferred (initial)** | **≤ 1.0MB** | Home, first viewport + critical CSS/JS |
| **JS (initial, gzipped)** | **≤ 120KB** | Defer non-critical; no heavy framework on content pages |
| **Web fonts** | **≤ 180KB** | Subset Latin + Bengali; see tactics |

**Tactics**
- **Critical CSS inlined** (<14KB) for the header + hero; defer the rest with `media="print"` swap or `rel="preload"` + async apply.
- **Fonts:** self-host WOFF2; `font-display: swap`; **subset** Inter & Playfair to Latin + needed punctuation, and Hind Siliguri / Noto Sans Bengali to the Bengali block — Bengali is loaded only on `/bn/` routes or when `:lang(bn)` is present. Preload only the two fonts used above the fold (`Playfair` H1 + `Inter` body, or their Bengali equals on `/bn/`).
- **Hero image:** `fetchpriority="high"`, preloaded via `<link rel="preload" as="image" imagesrcset=…>`; AVIF; sized to the actual rendered box (never a 1920px file on a 360px screen — `sizes` enforces this).
- **Lazy-load** all below-fold media and the map embed (Contact page) via `loading="lazy"` + an Intersection-Observer facade for the Google Map (load on interaction, not on paint).
- **No layout shift from fonts:** size-adjust/`@font-face` metric overrides on the swap fallback; reserve hero text height.
- **JS discipline:** drawer, carousel, language toggle, and accordions are vanilla/Islands-style — no SPA hydration on static content pages. Carousel uses native CSS scroll-snap (zero JS) with optional progressive-enhancement controls.
- **Connection-aware:** respect `prefers-reduced-data` / `navigator.connection.saveData` — skip the optional hero video/animation and serve a smaller `sizes` tier.
- **Preconnect** to the CDN/font origin; `dns-prefetch` for analytics; defer analytics until after LCP.

## 7. SEO & Technical Architecture

This section specifies the crawlable, performant, accessible, bilingual scaffolding that makes the brand strategy machine-readable. Every recommendation aligns to the foundation's compliant voice: schema and meta copy use "may support," "lifestyle education," and "consult your physician" — never "cure," "guaranteed," or any specialist title. ### 7.1 Semantic HTML5 Landmark Outline

One landmark set per page. Exactly one `<h1>`. Skip-link first in DOM. `lang` is set per language mirror (`bn` / `en`) and Bangla runs use explicit `lang` so screen readers switch voice.

```html

      <div class="lang-switch" role="group" aria-label="Language">
        <a lang="bn" hreflang="bn" href="/bn/">বাংলা</a>
        <a lang="en" hreflang="en" href="/en/" aria-current="true">EN</a>
      </div>
      <button aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="mobile-drawer" aria-label="Open menu">…</button>
    </nav>
  </header>

  </footer>

```

Rules: section landmarks (`<section>`, `<aside>`, `<nav>`) that repeat **must** carry a unique accessible name (`aria-label` or `aria-labelledby`). The compliance line lives in `<footer>` markup on every page (not injected by JS) so it is in the DOM at first paint and in the indexed source. Certification, disclaimer, and data blocks use `<aside>` or `<section>` with their own headings — never a styled `<div>` soup — so assistive tech and crawlers can locate the "trust" content the foundation prioritizes.

Lowercase, hyphenated, trailing-slash-consistent, directory-based, fully bilingual mirror. Canonical lives at the language-prefixed path; the root `/` 302s to the visitor's `Accept-Language` best match (default `/en/`).

### 7.5 JSON-LD Schema Recommendations Per Page Type

One `@graph` per page is preferred over multiple disconnected blocks. Map:

| Page | Primary types | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Home | `Organization` + `WebSite` (+ `SearchAction`) | Organization `founder` → Person; `WebSite` enables sitelinks search box. |
| About | `Person` + `MedicalBusiness` | Person credential = MBBS only. **No** `medicalSpecialty` claiming specialist status. |
| Contact | `MedicalBusiness` / `LocalBusiness` | Real Dhaka address, geo, hours, phone. |
| Pillar / Program | `WebPage` + `FAQPage` + `BreadcrumbList` | Program may use `Course` (educational framing) — never `MedicalProcedure`. |
| Blog article | `BlogPosting` + `BreadcrumbList` | `author` → the same Person `@id`. |
| Event (seminar) | `Event` (`EducationEvent`) | Public-education seminars; honest, dated. |
| Product | `Product` + `Offer` + (optional consented) `AggregateRating` | Certification surfaced via `additionalProperty`. |
| Testimonials | `Review` items only with consent | Reviews describe lifestyle improvement, never disease cure. |

Critical compliance guardrails for structured data:
- **`Person.hasCredential`** lists **only** MBBS. Never emit any postgraduate/specialist credential, `medicalSpecialty`, or any specialist `jobTitle`. `jobTitle` = "Lifestyle Medicine Educator."
- **`Review`/`AggregateRating`** must reflect genuine, consented feedback only; review `reviewBody` must describe lifestyle improvement, not disease cure — mirror the on-page "individual results vary" disclaimer.
- **`Product`** never carries health/therapeutic claims in `description`; use sourcing/certification facts.
- Do not emit `MedicalWebPage`/`Drug`/`MedicalCondition` schema asserting treatment outcomes.

### 7.6 Core Web Vitals Targets & Tactics

Field-data targets (75th percentile, mobile, 4G/3G common in audience): **LCP < 2.5 s · INP < 200 ms · CLS < 0.1.** Internal budgets: LCP < 2.0 s, INP < 150 ms, CLS < 0.05; total transfer < 500 KB on first view; JS < 120 KB gzip.

**LCP (hero podium image / H1):**
- Preload the hero image: `<link rel="preload" as="image" href="/img/hero-960.webp" imagesrcset="…" imagesizes="100vw" fetchpriority="high">`; set `fetchpriority="high"` on the LCP `<img>` and never lazy-load it.
- Self-host fonts (WOFF2, subset Latin + Bengali) with `font-display: swap` and preload only the two weights used above the fold; drop the render-blocking Google Fonts `<link>` currently in `index.html`.
- Inline critical CSS (~10–14 KB) for header + hero; defer the rest with `media="print" onload`. Brand colors as design tokens (`--green-900:#122319; --gold:#c9a05c; --cream:#f4efe4`).
- Cache-Control `immutable`, 1-year on hashed assets; Brotli compression; HTTP/2+; CDN edge near Dhaka.

**INP (mobile menu, accordions, language toggle):**
- Ship minimal vanilla JS; avoid hydration frameworks unless code-split. Break long tasks > 50 ms with `requestIdleCallback`/`scheduler.yield()`.
- Debounce search input (250 ms); `content-visibility: auto` on below-fold sections to cut layout/paint cost.
- Use CSS `:target`/`details`/`<dialog>` for the drawer where possible so interactions don't depend on JS.

**CLS (the killers here: web fonts, hero, ads-free but product images):**
- Every `<img>`/`<video>` has explicit `width`/`height` (or `aspect-ratio`) so the box is reserved — products are 1:1, OG 1.91:1, hero set by `aspect-ratio: 4/5` on mobile.

- Reserve space for the sticky header and the optional bottom action bar; never inject the compliance banner late.

### 7.9 Asset-Optimization Rules

Built for mobile users on variable Bangladeshi network speeds — payload discipline is a brand-trust feature, not just performance.

- **Formats:** AVIF → WebP → JPG fallback via `<picture>`; SVG for icons/logo/leaf motifs; no PNG for photos.

### 6.11 Implementation Guardrails (summary)

- **Gold discipline:** gold appears only as hairline rules, ≤24px icons, and primary CTAs. Never as a background fill on trust panels — heavy gold reads "luxury sales," undermining medical credibility.
- **Evidence zones stay cool/clean:** certifications, disclaimers, specs, and data on `--trust-white`/sage, never on rich brand green.
- **Disclaimers never `display:none`** at any breakpoint; min rendered size 13px; AA contrast enforced.
- **Reduced-motion:** count-ups, carousels, and hover filters all degrade to static.
- **Forbidden-credential lint** runs in CMS and CI — publishing is blocked on any disease-cure language or any title beyond the locked MBBS string.
- **Bilingual parity:** no trust element ships English-only; Bangla renders in Hind Siliguri / Noto Serif Bengali, never a fallback system font.

### 8. Form Best Practices

- **Single-column, ≤6 fields** for macro forms; **1–4** for micro. Label *above* field (faster mobile scanning), `16px` input font (prevents iOS zoom).
- Touch targets `≥48×48px`; field gap `16px`; full-width inputs on mobile.
- **Inline, real-time, polite validation** (validate on blur, not on every keystroke); errors in `#9a3b2e` *with* an icon + text (never color alone — accessibility).
- Correct mobile keyboards: `inputmode="tel"`, `type="email"`, `autocomplete` tokens.
- **Bilingual labels & placeholders**; error messages localized. Bengali rendered in Hind Siliguri / Noto Sans Bengali (never fallback).
- Required vs optional **explicitly marked**; keep optional fields minimal.
- Submit button: full-width mobile, shows loading state, disabled→enabled only when valid; **never** clears data on error.
- Consent: explicit, unticked, with linked Medical Disclaimer + Privacy Policy.
- Honeypot + time-trap for spam (no CAPTCHA friction unless abuse detected).

### 5. UX Recommendations

- **Mobile-first & thumb-friendly:** all interactive targets ≥ 44×44px; optional sticky bottom bar for Home/Programs/Shop/Contact; carousels use scroll-snap, not tiny arrows.
- **Performance on variable networks:** hero served as AVIF with WebP/JPEG fallback via `<picture>`, `fetchpriority="high"`, explicit `width/height` to prevent CLS; all below-fold imagery `loading="lazy"`; product rails lazy-load; total first-load JS ≤ 90 KB; self-host fonts with `font-display:swap` and preload only the two hero faces (Playfair 600, Inter 400).
- **Bilingual handling:** `lang` and `dir` set correctly per locale; load Noto Serif Bengali (headings) + Hind Siliguri/Noto Sans Bengali (body) only on `/bn/`; language toggle preserves the current page (`/en/…` ↔ `/bn/…`) and is keyboard-operable.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.2 AA — body ≥ 16px; contrast: cream text on `--green-900` and ink on cream both exceed 4.5:1 (verify gold-on-green only for large text/rules, never small body); every image has descriptive alt ("Dr. Jahangir Kabir speaking at a health seminar"); icon-only buttons get `aria-label`; visible focus rings; tab order matches visual order; honors `prefers-reduced-motion` (disable parallax/fade-ins).
- **Trust-by-design:** disclaimers and certification get their own clean panels (sage/white), never grey 10px footnotes. The compliance banner is a designed element, not an afterthought.
- **Micro-interactions:** 150–300ms `transform`/`opacity` only; cards lift via shadow + border-color, not size. No autoplay video, no aggressive modals/pop-ups.
- **Content integrity:** every health-outcome mention on the page is paired with a visible "consult your physician" / "individual results vary" note.

# 8. About Page

## Purpose & Goals

The About page is the **credibility engine** of the entire site — the page every skeptical persona (Tanvir hunting for credentials, Dr. Anika verifying legitimacy) visits before they trust anything else. Its job is narrative trust, not conversion volume.

**Primary goals**
1. Establish authority from *true* facts only — MBBS (Chittagong Medical College, 2000), 25+ years in lifestyle medicine, 1,000,000+ people reached, founder of the five-organization ecosystem, published books, public seminars.
2. Tell a coherent story: physician → lifestyle-medicine educator → ecosystem founder, anchored in the Five Pillars philosophy.
3. Pre-empt the core objection ("another influencer selling miracle cures?") with a visible, honest **Transparency & Credentials** block and compliant tone.
4. Route warmed-up visitors to the next logical step (5 Pillars, Programs, Ecosystem, Contact) without hard-selling.

**Success metrics:** scroll depth ≥ 70%, ≥ 25% click-through to 5 Pillars / Programs / Contact, low bounce from organic "Dr. Jahangir Kabir" / "lifestyle medicine Bangladesh" searches, time-on-page ≥ 90s.

### 5. UX Recommendations

**Accessibility (critical).**
- Every input has a persistent visible `<label>` (`for`/`id`) — no placeholder-as-label. Required fields marked with text "(required)" + `aria-required`, not color alone.
- Focus rings: 2px gold `#c9a05c` outline + 2px offset, visible on all interactive elements; tab order matches DOM/visual order.
- Body & inputs ≥ 16px (prevents iOS zoom-on-focus). Touch targets ≥ 44×44px; tap rows in info rail full-width 48px.
- Contrast: cream text on `#122319` ≥ 12:1; body `#1f2a22` on white ≥ 13:1; sage helper text kept ≥ 4.5:1 (avoid pale grey). Icon-only buttons (WhatsApp/phone in chips) carry `aria-label`.
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: disable the success-checkmark animation and any scroll smoothing.

**Form validation (inline + on submit).**
- **Strategy:** validate on blur, re-validate on input *after* first error, never validate on keystroke for untouched fields. Errors render below the field, in `#a23b2e` (accessible red on white), prefixed with an alert icon and `aria-describedby` wiring; field border turns red and `aria-invalid="true"`.
- **On submit:** if invalid, focus + scroll to the first error and render an error summary at the top of the card (links jump to each field) — covers `error-feedback` + accessibility.
- **Real messages:** Name → *"Please enter your name."*; Phone → *"Enter a valid phone number, e.g. +8801XXXXXXXXX."*; Email → *"That email address looks incomplete."*; Consent → *"Please confirm you understand this is a lifestyle-education request."*; Partnership-company empty → *"Please add your company or clinic name."*
- Phone field auto-formats and shows the selected country flag; email is optional unless Partnership (then required for the quote reply).
- Inline character counter on the textarea turns gold at 90%, red at 100%.

**Submission & confirmation UX.**
- On click: button enters loading state — label → *"Sending…"*, spinner, `disabled`, `aria-busy="true"` (prevents double-submit; covers `loading-buttons`).
- **Success:** replace the card body (not a full reload) with an inline success panel — sage background, green check (respecting reduced-motion), the confirmation copy + reference number, and two follow-ups: "Message on WhatsApp" and "Back to Home." Fire analytics `appointment_request_submitted`. Send a confirmation email/WhatsApp template echoing the reference and the compliance line.
- **Failure (network/server):** keep all entered values intact, show the error summary copy, offer WhatsApp fallback. Never lose the user's input.
- Reserve vertical space for the success/error panel so swapping content causes no layout jump (`content-jumping`).

**Performance & resilience (variable BD networks).**
- Map iframe is **lazy-loaded** behind a static map image + "Load map" affordance (or `loading="lazy"` with a low-res placeholder) so the page is interactive before maps JS arrives.
- Seminar photo: WebP/AVIF, `srcset`, `loading="lazy"`, explicit `width/height` to prevent CLS.
- Form posts via progressive enhancement: works as a normal POST if JS fails; AJAX enhances it.
- Inputs use correct `inputmode`/`autocomplete` (`tel`, `email`, `name`, `organization`) for faster mobile entry.

**Trust & honesty.**
- Compliance note sits *inside* the form, above submit — designed as a real element, not a tiny footnote.
- Set expectations explicitly: "request, not a confirmed slot," "1–2 working days," "visits by appointment." No fake urgency, no countdowns.

**Bilingual.**
- Form labels, errors, placeholders, hours, and disclaimer all translated; language radio pre-set from site language. Bangla rendered in **Hind Siliguri / Noto Sans Bengali** (never a fallback). Date/number formats localized; phone country default 🇧🇩.

### 7. Responsive Behavior

**Breakpoints:** `≤599px` mobile · `600–1023px` tablet · `≥1024px` desktop. Type scale `clamp()` (e.g. H1 `clamp(1.75rem, 5vw, 3rem)`; H2 `clamp(1.375rem, 3.2vw, 2rem)`; body fixed 16–17px).

| Section | Mobile (≤599) | Tablet (600–1023) | Desktop (≥1024) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **§1 Hero** | Centered, H1 then sub-head; contact chips stack 1-col full-width (48px) | Left-aligned; chips in a 3-up row | Left-aligned within 8-col; chips inline row, gold rule left-anchored |
| **§2 Work area** | Single column — **form first**, info rail below; no stickiness | 1 column still, or form (60%) + rail (40%) if width allows; rail not sticky | 2-col grid `1.4fr / 0.85fr`; rail `sticky top:96px` |
| **§2A Form fields** | All fields full-width, stacked; segmented control becomes a full-width 3-row stacked toggle if labels wrap | Pair short fields 2-up (e.g. date + time window); segmented control inline | Pair short fields 2-up; comfortable 2-col within card |
| **§2B Info rail** | Sub-cards stack; seminar photo `4/3` full-width; tap rows 48px | Sub-cards may go 2-up | Single sticky column beside form |
| **§3 Map** | `aspect-ratio 4/3`, "Get directions" full-width button below | `16/9` | `16/9`, directions button auto-width right-aligned |
| **§4 Disclaimer** | Single column, generous padding `clamp(1.25rem,5vw,2.5rem)` | Single column, wider measure (≤70ch) | Centered block, max measure 70ch |
| **§5 FAQ** | Accordion, all collapsed | Accordion | Accordion or 2-col Q/A grid |
| **Sticky bottom bar** | **Visible** — WhatsApp · Call · Book, 56px, safe-area inset | Hidden (chips/rail suffice) | Hidden |
| **Header CTA** | In hamburger drawer (pinned bottom) | Visible in header | Visible in header |

**Reflow rules:** no horizontal scroll at 320px; the segmented control and any 2-up field pairs collapse to 1-up below 600px; map and photo use intrinsic `aspect-ratio` (no CLS); the info rail loses `position: sticky` below 1024px to avoid trapping content on short viewports; mobile sticky bottom bar respects `env(safe-area-inset-bottom)` and never overlaps the submit button (form bottom padding `+72px` on mobile).
